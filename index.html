About Me

Hi! My name is Carrie Bennette. Thanks for visiting my website. This site serves as a living portfolio of my research projects & data visualizations. Building this site (from scratch!) was also a fun way for me to flex my new web design muscles üí™.

As a kid, my favorite subjects were science and art. I loved formulating hypotheses and discovering new knowledge by testing them and I loved creating beautiful and emotionally engaging drawings. I won a few art competitions early on and briefly considered going to art school for college, but ultimately decided science was probably harder to fit into my life as a hobby. As an undergrad I studied biology and art history. I started my career as a research biostatistician, where I wrote a lot of code to analyze national surveys, healthcare claims, and other large datasets. During my PhD and postdoc I expanded my skillset to include economic evaluation, simulation modeling and decision analysis. I also got to spend a lot of time combining my love of scientific discovery & art by developing data visualizations to communicate the results of complex statistical and mathematical models to various stakeholders.

In 2015 I joined the CHOICE Institute at UW as faculty and then left academia for a start up in 2017. I spent several years at Flatiron Health analyzing electronic health records, most recently as a director of the machine learning team. In 2020 I decided to pursue a new adventure in global health research and will be joining the Institute for Disease Modeling as a senior research scientist in March. Please wish me luck on this next adventure!

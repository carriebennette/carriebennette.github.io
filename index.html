About Me

Hi! My name is Carrie Bennette. Thanks for visiting my website. This sites serves as a living collection of my research projects and portfolio of data visualizations. Building this site (from scratch!) was also a fun way for me to flex my web design muscles.

As a kid, my favorite subjects were science and art. I loved discovering new knowledge and making things pretty. I won a few art competitions early on and briefly considered going to art school for college, but ultimately decided science was probably harder to fit into my life as a hobby. As an undergrad I studied biology and art history. Then, after realizing I have a truly terrible long-term memory, I switched gears to focus on developing tangible skills and started learning to write code. After a year abroad, I started my career as a biostatistician, where I wrote lots of code to analyze national surveys, healthcare claims, and other large datasets to answer a range of clinical & health policy research questions. During my PhD I expanded my skillset to include economic evaluations and decision analytic models. I also got to spend a lot of time combining my love of science & art by developing data visualizations to communicate the results of complex statistical and mathematical models to stakeholders.

After completing my PhD I joined the CHOICE Institute at UW as faculty for a few years and then spent nearly 3 years at Flatiron Health analyzing electronic health records, most recently as a director of the machine learning team. In 2020 I decided to pursue a new adventure in global health and will be joining the Institute for Disease Modeling as a senior research scientist in March. Please wish me luck on this next adventure!
